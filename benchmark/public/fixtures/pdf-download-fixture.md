# PDF Download Benchmark Fixture

> 10 canonical economics papers used to measure open-access PDF download success rate.

## Paper 1: The Colonial Origins of Comparative Development: An Empirical Investigation

- **Authors**: Acemoglu, Daron; Johnson, Simon; Robinson, James A.
- **Year**: 2001
- **Journal**: American Economic Review
- **DOI**: 10.1257/aer.91.5.1369
- **Core Branch**: Institutions and governance

### Summary
Uses settler mortality as an instrument for institutional quality.

### Why This Paper Is Irreplaceable
Established the empirical case that institutions cause development.

## Paper 2: Minimum Wages and Employment: A Case Study of the Fast-Food Industry in New Jersey and Pennsylvania

- **Authors**: Card, David; Krueger, Alan B.
- **Year**: 1994
- **Journal**: American Economic Review
- **DOI**: 10.1257/aer.84.4.772
- **Core Branch**: Difference-in-differences

### Summary
Classic natural experiment on minimum wage effects.

### Why This Paper Is Irreplaceable
Pioneered the use of difference-in-differences in labor economics.

## Paper 3: Nudging Farmers to Use Fertilizer: Theory and Experimental Evidence from Kenya

- **Authors**: Duflo, Esther; Kremer, Michael; Robinson, Jonathan
- **Year**: 2011
- **Journal**: American Economic Review
- **DOI**: 10.1257/aer.101.6.2350
- **Core Branch**: Agricultural and rural development

### Summary
Field experiment on fertilizer adoption in Kenya.

### Why This Paper Is Irreplaceable
Demonstrated how behavioral nudges can improve technology adoption.

## Paper 4: Golden Eggs and Hyperbolic Discounting

- **Authors**: Laibson, David
- **Year**: 1997
- **Journal**: Quarterly Journal of Economics
- **DOI**: 10.1162/003355397555253
- **Core Branch**: Time inconsistency

### Summary
Introduced hyperbolic discounting into economics.

### Why This Paper Is Irreplaceable
Provided a tractable model of present bias.

## Paper 5: Prospect Theory: An Analysis of Decision under Risk

- **Authors**: Kahneman, Daniel; Tversky, Amos
- **Year**: 1979
- **Journal**: Econometrica
- **DOI**: 10.2307/1914185
- **Core Branch**: Prospect theory

### Summary
Foundational paper on behavioral decision making.

### Why This Paper Is Irreplaceable
Introduced prospect theory and loss aversion.

## Paper 6: The Central Role of the Propensity Score in Observational Studies for Causal Effects

- **Authors**: Rosenbaum, Paul R.; Rubin, Donald B.
- **Year**: 1983
- **Journal**: Biometrika
- **DOI**: 10.1093/biomet/70.1.41
- **Core Branch**: Matching and propensity scores

### Summary
Introduced the propensity score method.

### Why This Paper Is Irreplaceable
Central to modern causal inference with observational data.

## Paper 7: Synthetic Control Methods for Comparative Case Studies: Estimating the Effect of California's Tobacco Control Program

- **Authors**: Abadie, Alberto; Diamond, Alexis; Hainmueller, Jens
- **Year**: 2010
- **Journal**: Journal of the American Statistical Association
- **DOI**: 10.1198/jasa.2009.ap08746
- **Core Branch**: Synthetic control methods

### Summary
Introduced synthetic control methods.

### Why This Paper Is Irreplaceable
Created a widely used method for comparative case studies.

## Paper 8: Does Compulsory School Attendance Affect Schooling and Earnings?

- **Authors**: Angrist, Joshua D.; Krueger, Alan B.
- **Year**: 1991
- **Journal**: Quarterly Journal of Economics
- **DOI**: 10.2307/2937954
- **Core Branch**: Instrumental variables

### Summary
Used quarter of birth as an instrument for schooling.

### Why This Paper Is Irreplaceable
Classic application of IV in labor economics.

## Paper 9: How Much Should We Trust Differences-in-Differences Estimates?

- **Authors**: Bertrand, Marianne; Duflo, Esther; Mullainathan, Sendhil
- **Year**: 2004
- **Journal**: Quarterly Journal of Economics
- **DOI**: 10.1162/003355304772839588
- **Core Branch**: Clustered inference

### Summary
Studied serial correlation in DID estimates.

### Why This Paper Is Irreplaceable
Changed standard practice for inference in DID designs.

## Paper 10: Measuring the Impacts of Teachers I: Evaluating Bias in Teacher Value-Added Estimates

- **Authors**: Chetty, Raj; Friedman, John N.; Rockoff, Jonah E.
- **Year**: 2014
- **Journal**: American Economic Review
- **DOI**: 10.1257/aer.104.9.2593
- **Core Branch**: Teacher value-added

### Summary
Evaluated bias in teacher value-added measures.

### Why This Paper Is Irreplaceable
Reassured the validity of teacher value-added for policy.
