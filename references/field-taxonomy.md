# Economics Field Taxonomy

> This reference defines the recognized economics subfields and their major branches. Use this taxonomy to ensure comprehensive coverage when curating a reading list. However, this is an internal reference, NOT a hard constraint — if the user's domain does not match any entry below, serve the request using model knowledge and thematic clustering instead.

## Classification System

Each subfield entry includes:
- **Core branches**: Major research areas within the subfield. A well-curated reading list should cover all core branches.
- **Neighboring subfields**: Related areas that share methods or questions.
- **Key handbooks/compendia**: Authoritative reference works for validation.

---

## A. Microeconomic Theory & Methods

### 1. Microeconomic Theory
**Core branches:** Consumer theory, producer theory, general equilibrium, decision under uncertainty, information economics (moral hazard, adverse selection), mechanism design, contract theory, social choice, matching theory
**Neighboring:** Game Theory, Mathematical Economics, IO, Behavioral, Public
**Key handbooks:** Handbook of Mathematical Economics (Vols. 1-4), Handbook of Game Theory (Vols. 1-4), Handbook of Social Choice and Welfare (Vols. 1-2)

### 2. Game Theory
**Core branches:** Non-cooperative games, cooperative games, repeated games, evolutionary game theory, bargaining, auctions, network games, epistemic game theory, stochastic games
**Neighboring:** Microeconomic Theory, IO, Political Economy, Experimental, Behavioral
**Key handbooks:** Handbook of Game Theory with Economic Applications (Vols. 1-4)

### 3. Macroeconomics
**Core branches:** Business cycles, economic growth (neoclassical, endogenous), monetary policy, fiscal policy, consumption and saving, investment, labor markets (search and matching), DSGE models, heterogeneous agent models (HANK), international macro
**Neighboring:** Monetary Economics, Growth, Labor, International Finance, Public
**Key handbooks:** Handbook of Macroeconomics (Vols. 1-2), Handbook of Monetary Economics (Vols. 1-3)

### 4. Monetary Economics
**Core branches:** Money demand and supply, inflation, central banking, monetary transmission, optimal monetary policy, liquidity and financial frictions, digital currency/CBDC, currency crises
**Neighboring:** Macroeconomics, Banking, International Finance
**Key handbooks:** Handbook of Monetary Economics (Vols. 1-3)

### 5. Economic Growth
**Core branches:** Neoclassical growth (Solow), endogenous growth (Romer, Lucas), unified growth theory, technology diffusion, human capital and growth, institutions and growth, middle-income trap, secular stagnation, creative destruction
**Neighboring:** Macroeconomics, Development, Innovation, Economic History
**Key handbooks:** Handbook of Economic Growth (Vols. 1-2)

### 6. Econometrics
**Core branches:** Linear regression and inference, instrumental variables, panel data (fixed effects, dynamic panels), time series (ARIMA, VAR, cointegration), limited dependent variables (logit, probit, tobit), nonparametric and semiparametric methods, Bayesian econometrics, GMM, bootstrap, program evaluation (DID, RDD, matching), machine learning in econometrics, high-dimensional methods
**Neighboring:** All applied fields, Statistics, Computer Science
**Key handbooks:** Handbook of Econometrics (Vols. 1-7)

### 7. Mathematical and Computational Economics
**Core branches:** Optimization theory, dynamic programming, stochastic processes, agent-based modeling, network theory, nonlinear dynamics and chaos, algorithmic game theory, machine learning applications
**Neighboring:** Micro Theory, Game Theory, Macroeconomics, Econometrics
**Key handbooks:** Handbook of Computational Economics (Vols. 1-4), Handbook of Mathematical Economics (Vols. 1-4)

## B. Applied Microeconomics: Labor, Public & Social Policy

### 8. Labor Economics
**Core branches:** Labor supply (static and life-cycle), human capital (Mincer, signaling), wage determination and inequality, unemployment and search/matching, discrimination, immigration, labor market institutions (minimum wage, unions), personnel economics, intergenerational mobility, local labor markets and spatial equilibrium, gig economy and alternative work arrangements
**Neighboring:** Public, Urban, Education, Health, Development
**Key handbooks:** Handbook of Labor Economics (Vols. 1-5)

### 9. Public Economics
**Core branches:** Optimal taxation (Mirrlees, Ramsey), tax incidence and behavioral responses, social insurance (UI, disability, pensions), public goods and externalities, fiscal federalism (Tiebout), government spending evaluation, political economy of policy, MVPF framework, tax evasion and enforcement, redistribution and universal basic income
**Neighboring:** Labor, Political Economy, Urban, Health
**Key handbooks:** Handbook of Public Economics (Vols. 1-5)

### 10. Health Economics
**Core branches:** Health production and demand (Grossman model), health insurance (moral hazard, adverse selection, ACA), provider behavior and physician agency, pharmaceuticals and innovation, health and labor market outcomes, health disparities, long-term care and aging, mental health economics, global health, healthcare systems comparison
**Neighboring:** Labor, Public, Development, Insurance
**Key handbooks:** Handbook of Health Economics (Vols. 1-3)

### 11. Education Economics
**Core branches:** Returns to education (Mincer, causal estimates), school quality and production functions, teacher labor markets and value-added, school choice and competition (vouchers, charters), early childhood education, higher education (college premium, student loans), peer effects, education and inequality, class size effects
**Neighboring:** Labor, Public, Urban, Development
**Key handbooks:** Handbook of the Economics of Education (Vols. 1-5)

### 12. Population and Family Economics
**Core branches:** Fertility and family planning, marriage markets and divorce, household bargaining and intrahousehold allocation, gender economics, migration (internal and international), demographic transition, economics of aging, child development and parenting
**Neighboring:** Labor, Development, Health, Education
**Key handbooks:** Handbook of Population and Family Economics (Vols. 1-2), Handbook of the Economics of Population Aging

### 13. Law and Economics
**Core branches:** Property rights (Coase theorem), tort law and liability, contract law, criminal law and deterrence, litigation and settlement, judicial behavior, regulation and administrative law, intellectual property law, corporate law and governance
**Neighboring:** IO, Public, Political Economy, Institutional Economics
**Key handbooks:** Handbook of Law and Economics (Vols. 1-2)

### 14. Economics of Crime
**Core branches:** Deterrence theory (Becker model), policing and crime, incarceration and recidivism, gun policy, drug policy, domestic violence, organized crime, white-collar crime, criminal justice system
**Neighboring:** Labor, Law and Economics, Public, Urban, Behavioral
**Key handbooks:** Featured in Handbook of Labor Economics and Handbook of Law and Economics

---

## C. Spatial, Urban & Regional Economics

### 15. Urban and Regional Economics
**Core branches:** Urban spatial structure (monocentric model, AMM), agglomeration economies, housing markets (supply, demand, affordability), transportation and commuting, local public finance, neighborhood effects and segregation, urban amenities and quality of life, regional growth and convergence, place-based policies, zoning and land use regulation, quantitative spatial models
**Neighboring:** Labor, Public, Trade, Real Estate
**Key handbooks:** Handbook of Regional and Urban Economics (Vols. 1-5)

### 16. Real Estate and Transport Economics
**Core branches:** Housing prices and cycles, mortgage markets, commercial real estate, real estate finance (REITs), land value and taxation, transport infrastructure, congestion pricing, transit economics, logistics and supply chain
**Neighboring:** Urban, Finance, Public, IO
**Key handbooks:** Handbook of Regional and Urban Economics (Vols. 1-5), Handbook of Transport Economics

---

## D. Industrial Organization & Innovation

### 17. Industrial Organization
**Core branches:** Market structure and market power, pricing strategies (price discrimination, bundling), product differentiation, entry and exit, antitrust and competition policy, platform and two-sided markets, empirical IO (BLP demand estimation, production function), auctions and procurement, vertical relationships, collusion and cartels, advertising
**Neighboring:** Innovation, Law and Economics, Labor, Trade
**Key handbooks:** Handbook of Industrial Organization (Vols. 1-4)

### 18. Innovation Economics
**Core branches:** R&D and productivity, patents and intellectual property, knowledge spillovers, geography of innovation, creative destruction (Schumpeterian growth), innovation policy (R&D tax credits, grants), entrepreneurship, diffusion of technology, science of science
**Neighboring:** IO, Growth, Urban, Labor, Corporate Finance
**Key handbooks:** Handbook of the Economics of Innovation (Vols. 1-2)

### 19. Economics of Digitization and Platforms
**Core branches:** Platform economics (network effects, multi-sided markets), digital markets and antitrust, AI and automation, data economics, privacy, gig economy, blockchain and crypto, digital advertising, e-commerce
**Neighboring:** IO, Innovation, Labor, Monetary
**Key handbooks:** Emerging field — see IO and Innovation handbooks

---

## E. International Economics

### 20. International Trade
**Core branches:** Comparative advantage (Ricardo, H-O), gravity models and trade costs, gains from trade, trade and labor markets, trade policy (tariffs, NTBs, trade agreements), firm heterogeneity and trade (Melitz), global value chains, trade and development, trade geography, services trade
**Neighboring:** IO, Labor, Development, Growth
**Key handbooks:** Handbook of International Economics (Vols. 1-5)

### 21. International Finance / Open Economy Macro
**Core branches:** Exchange rate determination, capital flows and sudden stops, sovereign debt, currency crises, international risk sharing, global imbalances, international monetary system, financial contagion, optimal currency areas
**Neighboring:** Macroeconomics, Monetary, Trade, Banking
**Key handbooks:** Handbook of International Economics (Vols. 1-5)

---

## F. Development & Agriculture

### 22. Development Economics
**Core branches:** Growth and convergence, poverty and inequality measurement, human capital (health, education in developing countries), agricultural and rural development, urbanization and structural transformation, institutions and governance, credit markets and microfinance, social networks and risk-sharing, conflict and development, RCT and experimental methods
**Neighboring:** Labor, Political Economy, Economic History, Public, Growth
**Key handbooks:** Handbook of Development Economics (Vols. 1-5)

### 23. Agricultural Economics
**Core branches:** Agricultural production and productivity, farm household models, food security and nutrition, agricultural markets and supply chains, land markets and tenure, agricultural policy and subsidies, rural development, climate and agriculture, technology adoption (Green Revolution, GMOs), commodity markets
**Neighboring:** Development, Environmental, Trade, IO
**Key handbooks:** Handbook of Agricultural Economics (Vols. 1-5)

---

## G. Environmental, Energy & Resources

### 24. Environmental Economics
**Core branches:** Externalities (Pigou, Coase), non-market valuation (hedonic, travel cost, contingent valuation), cost-benefit analysis, environmental regulation (taxes vs. cap-and-trade, CAFE standards), pollution and health, environmental justice, international environmental agreements, biodiversity and ecosystem services
**Neighboring:** Energy, Climate, Health, Public, Urban
**Key handbooks:** Handbook of Environmental Economics (Vols. 1-4)

### 25. Energy Economics
**Core branches:** Energy demand and supply, oil and gas markets (OPEC, fracking), electricity markets (deregulation, renewables integration), renewable energy, energy efficiency, energy and economic growth, energy security, fossil fuel subsidies and carbon lock-in
**Neighboring:** Environmental, Climate, IO, Natural Resource
**Key handbooks:** Handbook of Natural Resource and Energy Economics (Vols. 1-3)

### 26. Climate Change Economics
**Core branches:** Social cost of carbon (SCC, IAMs), climate policy (carbon pricing, green industrial policy), climate adaptation, climate migration, climate risk and financial markets, discounting and intergenerational equity, technology and energy transition, international climate agreements (Paris), climate damages and tipping points
**Neighboring:** Environmental, Energy, Growth, Public, Development
**Key handbooks:** Emerging field — see Environmental and Energy handbooks

### 27. Natural Resource Economics
**Core branches:** Renewable resources (fisheries, forests), non-renewable resources (Hotelling rule, mining), water economics, land use, resource curse, sustainability, biodiversity economics
**Neighboring:** Environmental, Energy, Agricultural, Development
**Key handbooks:** Handbook of Natural Resource and Energy Economics (Vols. 1-3)

---

## H. Behavioral & Experimental

### 28. Behavioral Economics
**Core branches:** Heuristics and biases (Tversky-Kahneman), prospect theory and loss aversion, time inconsistency and hyperbolic discounting, social preferences (altruism, reciprocity, fairness), nudge and choice architecture, limited attention and salience, behavioral public economics, behavioral finance, behavioral labor economics, behavioral development
**Neighboring:** All applied fields, Psychology, Neuroscience
**Key handbooks:** Handbook of Behavioral Economics (Vols. 1-3)

### 29. Experimental Economics
**Core branches:** Laboratory experiments, field experiments, artefactual and framed experiments, preference elicitation (risk, time, social), market experiments, game theory experiments, public goods and cooperation experiments, auction experiments, methodology (deception, incentives, external validity)
**Neighboring:** Behavioral, Game Theory, IO, Public, Development
**Key handbooks:** Handbook of Experimental Economics (Vols. 1-2)

---

## I. Political Economy, History & Institutions

### 30. Political Economy
**Core branches:** Voting and elections (median voter, probabilistic voting), political institutions and economic outcomes, corruption and rent-seeking, media and information, lobbying and campaign finance, bureaucracy and state capacity, historical political economy, redistribution and political conflict, populism, identity politics
**Neighboring:** Public, Development, Economic History, Institutional Economics
**Key handbooks:** Handbook of Political Economy

### 31. Economic History
**Core branches:** Long-run economic growth (Great Divergence), Industrial Revolution, colonialism and its legacies, financial history and crises, persistence and path dependence, historical institutions (AJR thesis), historical demography, cliometrics, history of economic thought
**Neighboring:** Development, Political Economy, Growth, Labor
**Key handbooks:** Handbook of Historical Economics, Handbook of Cliometrics

### 32. Institutional Economics
**Core branches:** Property rights and contracting, transaction costs, institutional change and persistence, formal vs. informal institutions, legal origins, culture and economics, social norms, state capacity
**Neighboring:** Political Economy, Economic History, Law and Economics, Development
**Key handbooks:** Handbook of New Institutional Economics

---

## J. Finance

### 33. Asset Pricing
**Core branches:** Portfolio theory (CAPM, APT), factor models (Fama-French, momentum, q-factor), equity premium puzzle, consumption-based asset pricing, behavioral asset pricing, term structure of interest rates, derivatives pricing (Black-Scholes), market efficiency and anomalies, liquidity and asset pricing, macro-finance
**Neighboring:** Macroeconomics, Corporate Finance, Econometrics, Monetary
**Key handbooks:** Handbook of the Economics of Finance (Vols. 1-2)

### 34. Corporate Finance
**Core branches:** Capital structure (Modigliani-Miller, trade-off, pecking order), payout policy, corporate governance and control, M&A, IPOs and security issuance, venture capital and private equity, corporate innovation, ESG and corporate social responsibility, executive compensation, corporate taxation
**Neighboring:** Asset Pricing, Banking, Innovation, IO, Law and Economics
**Key handbooks:** Handbook of the Economics of Corporate Finance, Handbook of the Economics of Finance (Vols. 1-2)

### 35. Banking and Financial Intermediation
**Core branches:** Financial intermediation theory, bank runs and systemic risk, bank regulation (Basel), relationship banking, shadow banking, fintech and digital banking, credit markets, monetary policy transmission through banks, financial crises
**Neighboring:** Corporate Finance, Monetary, Macroeconomics, Asset Pricing
**Key handbooks:** Handbook of Financial Intermediation and Banking

### 36. Market Microstructure
**Core branches:** Bid-ask spreads and liquidity, order flow and price discovery, high-frequency trading, market design and trading mechanisms, dealer markets, dark pools, exchange competition, information and market efficiency
**Neighboring:** Asset Pricing, IO, Banking
**Key handbooks:** Handbook of Market Microstructure (emerging)

### 37. Household Finance
**Core branches:** Portfolio choice and participation, mortgage choice, retirement savings, financial literacy, consumer credit and debt, behavioral household finance, insurance decisions, fintech and household finance
**Neighboring:** Asset Pricing, Banking, Behavioral, Labor, Public
**Key handbooks:** Handbook of Household Finance (emerging)

### 38. Insurance and Risk Economics
**Core branches:** Risk and uncertainty (Knight, Ellsberg), insurance demand theory, adverse selection and moral hazard in insurance markets, health insurance, life insurance, catastrophe risk, reinsurance markets, risk management
**Neighboring:** Health, Behavioral, Banking, Asset Pricing
**Key handbooks:** Handbook of Insurance, Handbook of the Economics of Risk and Uncertainty

## L. Income, Welfare & Social Economics

### 43. Income Distribution and Inequality
**Core branches:** Wage inequality (skill-biased technical change, trade, institutions), top income shares (Piketty-Saez), wealth inequality, intergenerational mobility, global inequality, inequality and growth, measurement of inequality (Gini, Theil, percentile ratios), polarization
**Neighboring:** Labor, Public, Macroeconomics, Growth, Political Economy
**Key handbooks:** Handbook of Income Distribution (Vols. 1-2)

### 44. Social Choice and Welfare Economics
**Core branches:** Social welfare functions, voting paradoxes (Arrow, Condorcet), axiomatic bargaining, fairness and distributive justice, poverty measurement, multidimensional welfare, happiness economics, capability approach
**Neighboring:** Public, Micro Theory, Game Theory, Political Economy
**Key handbooks:** Handbook of Social Choice and Welfare (Vols. 1-2)

### 45. Economics of Social Networks
**Core branches:** Network formation, diffusion and peer effects, social learning, network centrality and influence, labor market networks, financial networks and contagion, social capital
**Neighboring:** Labor, Development, IO, Game Theory, Computational
**Key handbooks:** Handbook of Social Economics (Vols. 1-2)

### 46. Economics of Religion
**Core branches:** Religion and economic growth (Weber thesis), religious participation and human capital, religion and political economy, religious markets and competition, religion and social insurance, secularization
**Neighboring:** Cultural, Development, Political Economy, Economic History
**Key handbooks:** Emerging field — see Handbook of the Economics of Religion

### 47. Economics of Philanthropy and Nonprofits
**Core branches:** Charitable giving, altruism and warm glow, nonprofit organizations, crowdfunding, corporate philanthropy, foundation economics, social enterprise
**Neighboring:** Public, Behavioral, Development
**Key handbooks:** Handbook of the Economics of Giving, Altruism and Reciprocity (Vols. 1-2)

## Selection Implications

When curating a reading list:
1. Cover all core branches — each should be represented by at least one paper.
2. Balance methods — include theoretical, structural, reduced-form, and experimental approaches.
3. Balance eras — foundational classics and recent breakthroughs.
4. Account for field evolution — focus on the active frontier, not dead branches.
5. For fields not listed here, use thematic clustering with the same principles.

**Remember: This taxonomy is a guide, not a cage. If the user asks for a field not listed, use your knowledge to serve them.**
