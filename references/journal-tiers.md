# Journal Tier Classification for Economics and Finance

> This reference classifies journals by tier and subfield relevance. Use it to understand publication venue hierarchy when curating reading lists. Remember: judge the paper, not the journal — but publication in top venues is itself a significant signal of quality and impact.

---

## Selection Guidelines

1. **T5 papers are always strong candidates** — Publication in a T5 is itself a signal of importance and broad impact.
2. **Field tops carry more weight within their subfield** — A JUE paper may be more central to urban economics than a tangentially related QJE paper.
3. **Finance Top 3 (JF, JFE, RFS) are T5-equivalent for financial economics** — Treat them with the same weight as T5 when curating finance-related lists.
4. **Do not exclude papers solely based on journal tier** — Some foundational papers appear in lower-ranked journals. Judge the paper, not the venue.
5. **Interdisciplinary journals** (Science, Nature, PNAS) occasionally publish influential economics papers — include only if the paper has become canonical within economics.
6. **Chinese journals**: Engage only when the user opts in via Chinese-language prompt. When included, prioritize 经济研究 and 经济学（季刊）as the most methodologically rigorous Chinese venues.
7. **SSCI Q1 and ABS 3+**: Journals in Tier 2-4 with SSCI Q1 or ABS 3/4/4* ratings are all considered high-quality venues. Higher ABS ratings (4 or 4*) carry stronger signals.
